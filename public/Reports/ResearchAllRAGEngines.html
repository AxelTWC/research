RAG Benchmark Project Report
Haystack vs RAGFlow vs LightRAG vs Dify vs LLMWiki

This benchmark suite compares five RAG systems under the same contract: identical corpus, scoring rules, prompt policy, and export schema. LLMWiki is the deliberately-simple Andrej Karpathy baseline (BM25 over the shared corpus + LLM, no embeddings, no reranker) used as a reference point against the full retrieval stacks. The goal is a fair, reproducible quality and performance comparison.

Created and maintained by Axel - Fujifilm.

Author: Axel - Fujifilm Datasets: TriviaQA RC + DuReader checklist + DocVQA Sample count: 100 Generation model: qwen/qwen3.5-35b-a3b Embedding model: text-embedding-qwen3-embedding-0.6b Reranker: qwen3-reranker-0.6b Backend: LM Studio (OpenAI-compatible) Latest DuReader update: 20260603

Shared Benchmark Contract

  • All frameworks use the same 100-sample shared corpus file and deterministic benchmark settings.
  • Same answer prompt behavior and same normalization/alias matching logic for EM/F1.
  • Same DeepEval metric schema: Answer Relevancy and Faithfulness.
  • Same output schema for full JSON, simplified JSON, CSV, and dashboard artifacts.

Interpretation Notes

  • Dify, Haystack, and RAGFlow all produced usable outcomes under the shared contract.
  • RAGFlow leads on raw quality metrics, while Haystack leads on total-time efficiency; Dify remains competitive.
  • LightRAG currently underperforms in this benchmark context and requires stabilization before decision use.
  • LLMWiki (Karpathy method) — a no-embedding, no-reranker BM25 baseline — actually leads on EM/F1 (EM 0.69, F1 0.79) and matches the heavier stacks on Answer Relevancy and Faithfulness, with the lowest total runtime by a wide margin. Strong evidence that on this corpus the heavier retrieval stacks add little quality over a well-built lexical baseline.
  • DuReader update (20260528): Haystack outperforms LLMWiki across EM/F1/Relevancy/Faithfulness (EM +0.04, F1 +0.0369, Relevancy +0.0427, Faithfulness +0.0128), with lower TTFT and total runtime; LLMWiki remains higher in TPS.
  • DuReader update (20260603): RAGFlow-DuReader enters the suite. RAGFlow leads all three on Relevancy (0.8545) and Faithfulness (0.9150) and edges Haystack on F1 (0.5889 vs 0.5882), but has the highest TTFT (1.15s) and total runtime (125.1s). Haystack remains the fastest at 77.9s with near-identical quality to RAGFlow. LLMWiki maintains the highest TPS (7398) but trails on every quality metric.
  • The benchmark structure is now reusable and portable across machines.
